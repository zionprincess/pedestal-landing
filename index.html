The gap

English-only evaluation misses what your audience actually said.

Models trained and scored mostly on English content quietly mistranslate, misclassify, or wave through unsafe content in local languages. That gap costs trust, and it's invisible until someone in-market catches it.

01

Code-switching breaks scoring

Real Nigerian content mixes English, Pidgin, and mother tongue mid-sentence. Rubrics built for one language misscore the rest.

02

Tone gets flattened

Sarcasm, proverbs, and register in Yoruba or Igbo often read as neutral or wrong to models with no local grounding.

03

Safety checks fall through

Harmful or sensitive content phrased in Hausa or Pidgin slips past filters tuned only for English patterns.

The layer

One correction layer, four languages, built by native evaluators.

PEDESTAL sits between your content pipeline and your model, catching what generic evaluation misses before it ships.

Ingest

Content comes in as-is

Pidgin, Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa, or naturally code-switched text — no pre-translation, no stripped context.

Evaluate

Native evaluators score against a real rubric

Severity classification, inter-rater reliability checks, and adjudication on disputed calls, the same rigor as any English-language QA workflow.

Correct

Corrections feed back into your pipeline

Flagged errors and fixes return as structured data your model or dataset can actually learn from.
